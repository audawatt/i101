West Lafayette, IN

I'm from West Lafayette, Indiana. It is mainly known for being home to Purdue University. The city is built along the banks of the Wabash River, and its sister city Lafayette is on the other side. The river is kind of gross so there's no point in swimming in it, but it's fun to go canoeing in. It's conveniently located about an hour from Indy, two hours from Bloomington, and two and a half hours from Chicago. Overall, West Lafayette is kind of boring, but it's home and I like living there.

Purdue University

Place

Purdue University is one of the nation's top colleges and research universities. It's ranked among the top ten public universities. It was established on May 6, 1869. It's mainly known for engineering, nursing, and aviation. Their mascots are the Boilermaker Special and Purdue Pete. The Special is a train but it's really a car built to look like a train. I've seen people driving it around campus, and also on the highway near my house.

Feast of the Hunter's Moon

Event

The Feast of the Hunter's Moon takes place every October at Fort Ouiatenon, the first European outpost in modern-day Indiana. The fort is located about four miles southwest of West Lafayette. The feast is a re-enactment of a historic annual gathering between the French and Native Americans. This year it's happening on October 5-6. There are lots of fun activies, like axe-throwing, blanket weaving, and candle dipping. There's also authentic Native American and French food and traditional music.

Sheila Klinker

Person

Sheila Klinker is a member of the House of Represenatives and represents both the W. Lafayette and Lafayette districts. She graduated from Purdue with a bachelor's in education and taught for nearly forty yeasr in the Lafayette School Corporation before entering politics. I've actually met her and talked to her on more than one occasion because somehow she finds time to go to literally every public event. I admire her dedication to her people and the city.

Personal Connection

I was born in Kalamazoo, MI, but I moved to West Lafayette when I was six. I consider West Lafayette my home because it's where all the important people in my life are from and it's where most of my childhood memories take place. My dad works at Purdue, and the campus being only fiteen minutes away meant I could go often. I was a part of Purdue's Summer Band, and attended a lot of Purdue camps in my youth. In fact, it was a women in cybersecurity camp that got me interested in my current major, and it's the reason I'm doing CSGP now. West Lafayette is my home and it's a special place to me.
